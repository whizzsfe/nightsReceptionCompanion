WHAT IS THE NIGHT AUDIT?
The night audit is the financial reconciliation process that closes the hotel's business day. It posts all outstanding room charges, reconciles payments, and generates management reports. It is one of the most important responsibilities of the night receptionist — and a great career skill to master.

⭐ NIGHT HANDOVER BEST PRACTICES
FOR NEW HIRES
Lead with what's urgent. The morning team walks in ready for breakfast rush — tell them immediately about any ongoing complaints, guests who are difficult, or anything that needs action before 8am.
Flag all card / payment issues. Any declined pre-auths, billing disputes raised overnight, or folios that need attention — list them clearly. The morning manager needs to know before the guest calls again.
Report all incidents, even minor ones. That guest who was mildly unhappy at 2am might call to complain at 9am. The morning team must never be caught off guard. If it happened, it goes in the handover.
Note the night audit outcome. Completed cleanly? Any discrepancies? Reports printed? Even "audit ran cleanly, no issues" is valuable to write — it confirms the task was done.

EXCELLENCE TIPS FOR THE NIGHT SHIFT
🌙
You Are the Whole Hotel Tonight
At night, there's no manager to pass the problem to, no colleague to ask. That sounds daunting, but it's actually your greatest opportunity. Every situation you handle alone builds confidence no training course can give you.
💳
Card Policy — Hold the Line, Keep the Warmth
The card-only rule protects you and the hotel. When a guest pushes back, your tone matters as much as the policy. Be firm but never cold. "I'm sorry, it's a property-wide rule and not something I can change, but let me see what I can do to help" goes a long way.
📝
If It's Not Written Down, It Didn't Happen
Every incident, request, and call at night must be logged. Not just for the handover — for your protection. If something escalates later and there's no record, the blame can land on the night shift. Log everything, every time, with a time stamp.
🛡️
Own the Problem, Not the Blame
When a guest has a complaint, your job is not to explain why it happened — it's to fix it, or make them feel heard until it can be fixed. "I will personally make sure this is followed up by morning" is honest and powerful. Follow through is everything.
🔒
Security Is a Service
Every challenge to an unknown person, every locked door check, every CCTV glance — that's hospitality. Guests sleep soundly because you're there. Never feel like security tasks are beneath the role. They're one of the most valuable things you do.
🧠
Night Audit Is Your Superpower
Many receptionists never master the night audit. Those who do stand out massively on their CV and in promotions. Learn every step, understand why each one matters, and you'll be ahead of most front desk staff within a year.
🌙
YOU ARE BUILDING SOMETHING RARE
Night shift experience is some of the most valuable in hospitality. You handle situations most receptionists never face, you know the hotel inside-out, and you do it alone. That makes you exceptional — and every manager who's worked nights knows it.
